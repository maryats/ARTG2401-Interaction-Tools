"There the form stood..."
Linocut print.

This print was inspired by passages in Thomas Hardyâ€™s Return of the Native:

"There the form stood, motionless as the hill beneath. Above the plain rose the hill, above the hill rose the barrow, and above the barrow rose the figure. Above the figure was nothing that could be mapped elsewhere than on a celestial globe."

"... A change took place in the mass of shade which denoted the distant landscape. Red suns and tufts of fire one by one began to arise, flecking the whole country round. They were the bonfires of other parishes and hamlets that were engaged in the same sort of commemmoration..."

Process

This project was my first foray into linoleum printmaking, and enabled me to play around with different color combinations as a way of conveying different atmospheres.
